the engineering thesis

What it costs, and what it takes

The economics and discipline behind the thesis: total cost of ownership, the quality and security of LLM-authored code, governing a model inside a harness you don't own, and the drift it fights. Every external claim is cited; every objection is answered.

LLM-First by design

The 90 : 10 target

Reasoning as the default operational layer; hand-authored code the exception, for what a model should not do (irreversible, identity, money, secrets). A build posture too: author blueprints + intent, a governed compiler emits behavior, a human curates.

LLM-First · target

90% AI · 10% code

Governed reasoning carries operational behavior; a thin deterministic shell holds the irreversible parts. Evolved from BOSNet.io's original 80/20.

LLM-Heavy · off target

Many calls, still code-first

An LLM bolted onto code-first structure, with no governed default and no compiler. Lots of AI; none of the substrate.

total cost of ownership

Maintenance is where LLM-First wins

Software is paid for over its life, not when written. The 60/60 rule: ~60% of lifetime cost is maintenance, ~60% of that is enhancement. Moving operational logic to blueprints changes what you maintain for a decade.

~60%

Of lifetime cost is maintenance: the part LLM-First attacks.

70-85%

Of hand-authored surface is operational logic: the compile target.

15-30%

Irreducible plumbing floor (auth, billing, secrets, RLS) that stays code.

LLM-authored code

A draft until something enforces it

Every line here is LLM-authored; Rob is the human-in-the-loop orchestrator. The published evidence on ungoverned LLM code is not flattering, so the code is held to a bar.

~45%

Of security-relevant tasks, models pick the insecure option (Veracode 2025).

19%↓

Experts were slower with AI while feeling faster (METR).

15-50

Defects/KLOC baseline for typical dev (McConnell).

  • Pre-commit guards, a chained set: endpoint-lock, a no-stubs gate, blueprint-lint, a public-view protocol, a content-truth check.
  • CI, in order: typecheck → lint → test → build, a hard service-role-key guard, ephemeral-DB pgTAP + RLS audits.
  • Audit-style remediation: numbered findings closed in tracked batches (F-01…F-12), IDOR/authz fixes, committed-secret cleanup.
The claim: governed LLM code, held to these gates by a disciplined orchestrator, is a deliverable, and the governance is the product.
a harness you don't own

Cooperative, always-on, and its ceiling

Building with Claude.ai Max means governing a model inside someone else's harness. You can't modify the runtime, so governance is a cooperative overlay that re-asserts every session: session mandates → tool-call hooks → a pre-impl architecture-fit check → a post-impl drift audit → self-heal.

But be honest about the ceiling: an overlay is only as good as what hasn't changed in the harness beneath it and what you didn't forget to invoke. A rule not loaded, a hook not registered, is not enforced. Omission is ungoverned. Managing drift dies here first.
drift & the substrate

Code, harness, and knowledge

Two drift modes come from a model trained on code-first software (code-first drift; and harness-injection, where it smuggles governance logic into the app schema). The third has nothing to do with code: knowledge drift: the wrong, stale, or missing context. Prompts govern what the model may do; they don't supply what it knows.

The remedy is a governance-carrying dimensional matrix: naive vector RAG returns what's near, not what's governed and true (RAGTruth, Lost in the Middle, OWASP LLM08). The rule: right index per query class.

The operational substrate of an LLM is governance plus knowledge. Drift is the erosion of either half: a rule un-invoked, a harness shifted, or context fed wrong. Managing it means holding both, continuously.
objections we take seriously

The sharpest counter-arguments, answered

ObjectionAnswer
"Compounding error dooms run-time reasoning."99%/step → ~37% over 100 steps. Answer: the auditor re-derives ground truth and discards failing steps, so per-step correctness is enforced by code rather than left to decay. Agency is concentrated in about 8 decisions rather than 100, and rejects are thrown away, never compounded. (agent-eval survey)
"The governance/knowledge framing hides a false trichotomy."Policy and knowledge are authored artifacts too. Answer: we don't claim code disappears; the locus of authored truth shifts to declarative governed artifacts, and decision execution shifts to run-time. That's a real difference in what you maintain and when decisions are made. (policy-as-code · GraphRAG-is-RAG)
"Structure doesn't always pay."Graph knowledge can lose to vector RAG on factoids and costs more. Answer: agreed: right index per query class. Governed structure is for what must be accountable; similarity search is fine for open factoid lookup. (RAG vs GraphRAG)
On the 90 : 10 number.It's a design aspiration. No study shows an optimal ratio; it's the target BOSNet.io reaches for, and each project approaches honestly (Zabble stays ~30-40). A measured north star.
governance built to free people

Governing AI begins with governing people

Safe, governed tools free people rather than constrain them, and the evidence backs it (DORA: lightweight guardrails beat heavyweight gates on speed and stability; NIST AI RMF makes GOVERN a people function). It holds under one condition: the guardrails act as paved roads, easy to follow, rather than fences that block movement.
