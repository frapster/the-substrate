six files driving the entire knowledge product

Reasoning is the knowledge-production substrate. It produces artifacts — KB rows, drafts — that people and deterministic code then review, persist, and serve. The model manufactures; the app delivers.
the ingestion pipeline

From raw manuscript to a queryable, cross-referenced knowledge base

Two stages are essentially pure reasoning: parse-and-embed, and LLM-judged de-duplication. Everything downstream — retrieval, publishing — is conventional code with a human gate.

Grounded + gated. Generation cites retrieved context, moderation runs on input and output, and nothing publishes until a human reviewer is stamped on the row.

how the substrate is built

Six load-bearing pieces

01 · DIMENSIONAL MODEL

Four-facet knowledge base

Every source decomposes into prose · facts · qa · taxonomy, each with its own content JSON, provenance, ordering, and embedding. The facets are the dimensions.

02 · KNOWLEDGE GRAPH

Emergent semantic edges

A cross-ref table stores typed edges — duplicate, nuance_variant, contradicts, extends — discovered by the reasoning pass, not hand-curated.

03 · VECTORIZED DATA

Hybrid retrieval

pgvector + HNSW cosine fused with Postgres full-text via reciprocal rank fusion, behind a security-definer function. Returns numbered, citable context.

04 · GOVERNANCE

Grounding + moderation

Citation-required, no-fabrication prompts; input and output moderation; a documented security-hardening pass. Reasoning produces; guardrails constrain.

05 · CONTROL

Rate limit + breaker

Layered rate limiting and a site-wide circuit breaker sit in front of every reasoning endpoint. RLS and role checks everywhere.

06 · PUBLISH

Human-in-the-loop

Content moves draft → review → published; the reviewer is stamped on publish. People and code decide what goes live.
